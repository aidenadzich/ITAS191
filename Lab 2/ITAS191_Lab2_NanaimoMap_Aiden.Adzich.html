Details about Places

Nanaimo Museum

I used to go to the Nanaimo Museum quite frequently when I was younger, as it was a popular place for my homeschooling group to go on field trips. I haven't been in many years, but I clearly remember walking through the mine recreation and thinking it was the coolest thing ever.

Departure Bay Ferry

I love taking the ferry to Vancouver, and the most common place I leave from is Departure Bay. I often leave early in the morning, and return late at night, so I get to watch the sunrise and sunset from the ferry.

The Bastion

A small tower next to the museum built by The Hudson's Bay Company in 1853 to defend the coal operations in Nanaimo.

Woodgrove Mall

Woodgrove is in my opinion, the best mall on Vancouver Island. It's filled with a mix of of small stores and large brands, and it very rarely has empty spaces. I've spent so much time walking around the mall with my sister, and she constantly wants me to drive her to Nanaimo so we can go.

Island Highway

The old areas of the highway leading up The Island are among my favourite roads to drive, from Duncan to Courtenay, and the Island Highway going through Nanaimo is no different. It's a great drive that leads you right through the center of the city.
